Generate proven ad concepts for any product — 320+ templates organized by angle, audience, and platform.

## Part 1: Concept Generation Framework (Standalone)

### 1.1 The Concept Anatomy

Every ad concept has five elements:

```
CONCEPT
=======
Angle: [The strategic perspective — WHY someone should care]
Headline: [The words that stop the scroll — WHAT you're saying]
Visual: [The image/video direction — HOW it looks]
CTA: [The action step — WHAT to do next]
Audience: [WHO this speaks to specifically]
```

### 1.2 Universal Ad Angles

These 16 angles work across virtually every product category. Each generates dozens of concepts.

#### 1. Problem/Pain Point
Focus on the frustration the product solves.
- "Tired of [problem]?"
- "Still dealing with [pain point]?"
- "[Problem] is costing you [specific consequence]"

#### 2. Transformation/Result
Focus on the after-state — the outcome of using the product.
- "From [before] to [after] in [timeframe]"
- "What [X days/weeks] of [product] looks like"
- "[Result] without [sacrifice]"

#### 3. Social Proof
Let others do the convincing.
- "[X,000] customers can't be wrong"
- "Why [demographic] are switching to [product]"
- "[Star rating] with [number] reviews"

#### 4. Comparison/Alternative
Position against the current solution.
- "[Product] vs [competitor/old way]"
- "Still using [old solution]? There's a better way"
- "What I wish I knew before buying [competitor]"

#### 5. Urgency/Scarcity
Create time pressure to act now.
- "[X]% off ends [day]"
- "Only [X] left at this price"
- "Last chance: [specific offer]"

#### 6. Authority/Expert
Leverage credibility and expertise.
- "[Expert type] recommended"
- "The [product category] [professionals] actually use"
- "Backed by [research/institution]"

#### 7. Price/Value
Emphasize the financial proposition.
- "[Product] for less than [relatable daily expense]"
- "Replace your $[X] [thing] with this $[Y] alternative"
- "[Benefit] for just $[price]/[time period]"

#### 8. Curiosity/Intrigue
Create information gaps that demand clicking.
- "The [product type] they don't want you to know about"
- "Why [unexpected thing] is the key to [desired outcome]"
- "I wasn't supposed to share this, but..."

#### 9. Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)
Highlight what they're missing by not having it.
- "Everyone's talking about [product] — here's why"
- "Your [competitor's customers] already have this"
- "While you wait, [X] people just ordered"

#### 10. Lifestyle/Aspirational
Show the life the product enables.
- "This is what [aspirational routine] looks like"
- "[Product] for people who [aspirational trait]"
- "Level up your [area of life]"

#### 11. Education/How-To
Teach something valuable, product is the tool.
- "How to [achieve result] in [timeframe]"
- "[Number] [tips/hacks/mistakes] for [desired outcome]"
- "The beginner's guide to [topic]"

#### 12. Emotional/Story
Connect through narrative and feeling.
- "I almost gave up on [goal]. Then I found [product]"
- "My [family member] noticed the difference first"
- "The day I stopped [old behavior] changed everything"

#### 13. Seasonal/Timely
Tie to current events, seasons, or moments.
- "[Season] essentials you're missing"
- "New year, new [category]"
- "The [holiday] gift they actually want"

#### 14. Risk Reversal
Remove the barrier to trying.
- "Try it free. Seriously."
- "[X]-day money-back guarantee — no questions"
- "If you don't love it, we'll refund every penny"

#### 15. Ingredient/Feature Spotlight
Deep-dive on what makes it work.
- "The secret ingredient: [specific ingredient/feature]"
- "Why [feature] matters more than [commonly valued feature]"
- "What's actually inside [product]"

#### 16. User-Generated/Review Highlight
Feature real customer content.
- "[Customer name]: '[real review quote]'"
- "Real results from real [customers/users]"
- "What [customer] said after [X] [days/uses]"

### 1.4 Headline Formula Library

#### Proven Structures (fill in brackets)

**Benefit-Driven:**
- "Get [result] without [sacrifice]"
- "[Result] in [timeframe] or your money back"
- "The fastest way to [desired outcome]"

**Curiosity-Driven:**
- "The [adjective] truth about [topic]"
- "[Number] things you didn't know about [topic]"
- "What happens when you [action] for [timeframe]"

**Social Proof-Driven:**
- "Why [number] [people] switched to [product]"
- "[Star rating] — here's what customers are saying"
- "The [product category] [demographic] swears by"

**Comparison-Driven:**
- "[Product] vs [alternative]: the honest truth"
- "We tested [number] [products]. This one won."
- "The $[low] alternative to $[high] [brand/product]"

**Urgency-Driven:**
- "[Offer] ends [specific time]"
- "[Number] sold today — [number] remaining"
- "Your last chance to get [product] at [price]"

### 1.6 Concept Generation Process

Follow this process to generate concepts for any product:

```
1. RESEARCH (2 min)
   - What does the product do?
   - Who is the target audience? (demographics + psychographics)
   - What are the top 3 pain points it solves?
   - What is the price point? (affects which angles work)
   - What social proof exists? (reviews, stats, endorsements)

2. SELECT ANGLES (1 min)
   - Pick 3-5 angles from the 16 universal angles above
   - Prioritize: pain point, social proof, and transformation ALWAYS work
   - Add category-specific angles from section 1.3

3. GENERATE HEADLINES (2 min)
   - Write 2-3 headline variations per angle
   - Use the formula library in section 1.4
   - Test different tones: direct, curious, emotional, authoritative

4. SPECIFY VISUALS (1 min per concept)
   - Match visual style to angle (see section 1.5)
   - Include specific direction: subject, background, lighting, composition
   - Note any text overlay content and placement

5. ASSIGN AUDIENCE + CTA (30s per concept)
   - Who specifically would respond to this angle?
   - What's the logical next step? (shop, learn more, try free, etc.)

6. OUTPUT 5-10 CONCEPTS
   - Each fully specified with angle, headline, visual, CTA, audience
   - Ready for design/production
```

## Part 2: Produce Your Concepts

Turn concepts into production-ready assets at scale.

### Static Images
- **Product photography**: Use the Asset Generator with Nano Banana for product shots that match your visual direction. Best text rendering of any model.
- **Text-heavy ads**: Use Flux Pro for images with bold typography and design elements.
- **IAB banners**: Use the IAB Images endpoint to generate display banners in all standard sizes from a single product image.

### Video Versions
- **URL to Video**: Feed your product URL to the URL-to-Video endpoint — it creates a complete video ad including script, visuals, and CTA.
- **Custom scripts**: Use the AI Scripts endpoint with your headline/concept, then generate with AI Avatar for a talking-head version.

### Avatar Versions
- **Talking head ads**: Take any concept headline and turn it into a 15-30s avatar script. Use [ai-avatar-video](https://github.com/Creatify-AI/ai-avatar-video) for persona selection and script writing.

### Example: Concept to Production

```python
import requests

HEADERS = {
    "Content-Type": "application/json",
    "X-API-ID": "your-api-id",
    "X-API-KEY": "your-api-key",
}
BASE_URL = "https://api.creatify.ai/api"

# Turn a product image into IAB display banners
def concept_to_iab_banners(product_image_url):
    """Generate IAB banner ads from a product image."""
    resp = requests.post(f"{BASE_URL}/iab_images/", headers=HEADERS, json={
        "image": product_image_url,
    })
    return resp.json()

# Turn a concept into a video ad
def concept_to_video(product_url, aspect_ratio="9x16"):
    """Generate a video ad from a product URL."""
    link = requests.post(f"{BASE_URL}/links/", headers=HEADERS, json={
        "url": product_url,
    }).json()

    video = requests.post(f"{BASE_URL}/link_to_videos/", headers=HEADERS, json={
        "link_id": link["id"],
        "aspect_ratio": aspect_ratio,
    }).json()

    return video

# Turn a concept into an avatar ad
def concept_to_avatar(script, creator_id, aspect_ratio="9:16"):
    """Generate an avatar video from a concept script."""
    resp = requests.post(f"{BASE_URL}/lipsyncs/", headers=HEADERS, json={
        "text": script,
        "creator": creator_id,
        "aspect_ratio": aspect_ratio,
    })
    return resp.json()
```
